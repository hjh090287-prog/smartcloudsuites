🎓 How to Calculate Your NZ Salary (Manual)

1. The "Slice" System (PAYE Tax)

Governed by the Income Tax Act 2007, New Zealand uses a Progressive Tax System. This means your income is taxed in "slices" rather than a flat rate. As you earn more, only the money in the higher bracket is taxed at the higher rate.

  • • Your first $15,600 is taxed at 10.5%.
  • • Income between $15,601 and $53,500 is taxed at 17.5%.
  • • Only the money above $180,000 is taxed at the highest 39% rate.

View Income Tax Act 2007 →

2. ACC Earner's Levy (1.75%)

Under the Accident Compensation Act 2001, the ACC Levy is your mandatory no-fault injury insurance. In NZ, every worker contributes $1.75 for every $100 earned (up to a maximum threshold of $152,790). This ensures that if you are injured anywhere—at work, home, or play—ACC covers your medical costs and provides up to 80% of your income while you recover.

Learn how ACC is funded →

3. Student Loan & KiwiSaver

Student Loans: Governed by the Student Loan Scheme Act, you only start paying back your loan once your income exceeds the Repayment Threshold of $22,828. For every dollar earned over that, 12 cents is deducted.

KiwiSaver: Governed by the KiwiSaver Act 2006, your chosen percentage is deducted from your gross pay to save for your retirement or first home.
